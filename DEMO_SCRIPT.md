# 🎬 CodeMongUs — Split-Screen Demo Script 

**Target Time:** ~1 minute 45 seconds

---

## Part 1: The Intro & Login (Full Screen)

**[SCREEN: Show the Login Page. Keep talking while you type in your credentials.]**
> "Good morning judges! We are excited to present **CodeMongUs** — a real-time multiplayer game where coding meets social deduction. Our goal was to make pair-programming and DSA practice actually fun. Our system uses secure Firebase authentication, so let me quickly log in here and we can jump right into the action."

---

## Part 2: The Lobby & Transition (Split Screen)

**[SCREEN: Show the Dashboard. Create a room. Then visually snap to Split-Screen (Left: You, Right: Player 2)]**
> "As you can see, we’ve built a sleek, modern UI. As the host, I can easily create a new game room and configure settings like the coding language and the timer. 
> 
> To really show you how our sockets work, I’m using a split-screen view. On the left is me, Player 1. On the right is Player 2. As you can see, I just copied my room code and sent it to Player 2. Oh, there they are! They successfully joined my room. Now that we are both in the lobby, I will hit start."

**[SCREEN: Both screens show the Role Reveal animation]**
> "Once the game starts, both players instantly receive a secret role. Player 1 is our **Impostor**, and Player 2 is our **Crewmate**."

---

## Part 3: The Code Editor & Features (Full Screen -> Split Screen)

**[SCREEN: Maximize Player 1's window (Full Screen) for just a few seconds to show the clean UI]**
> "Once the coding phase begins, here is our full Code Editor dashboard. We wanted to make sure the interface was clean, modern, and distraction-free for solving the DSA problem."

**[SCREEN: Snap back to Split-Screen. Both players are in the editor. Move cursors so both are visible in real-time.]**
> "Now, snapping back to our split-screen view, you can see the real collaborative magic. As I type on the left, it instantly syncs to the right. The Crewmate is trying to solve the problem, but the Impostor is secretly trying to ruin the codebase without getting caught."

**[SCREEN: Use your mouse to point to the different features on the sidebar]**
> "We've added a **Live Chat** so players can communicate directly in the editor. Once the team thinks the solution is ready, they can propose to **Commit Code**. 
> 
> But notice the Impostor's screen on the left — they have secret **Sabotage Buttons**. With one click, they can scramble the code or blind the crewmates to cause maximum chaos during the coding phase."

---

## Part 4: The Meeting & Ejection (Split Screen)

**[SCREEN: Player 2 (Crewmate) clicks the "Report Bug" button. The Voting Modal pops up on both screens.]**
> "If a Crewmate suspects someone is messing up the code, they hit the **Report Bug** button. This pauses the game and calls an emergency meeting."

**[SCREEN: Show the Live Chat briefly during the meeting, then have both players vote to eject Player 1 (The Impostor)]**
> "Players can debate in the chat, and then cast their anonymous votes. In this case, Player 2 caught the Impostor red-handed! Both players lock in their votes, and the results are tallied instantly in real-time."

---

## Part 5: The Climax & Outro (Full Screen or Split)

**[SCREEN: The Animation of Player 1 being ejected pops up, followed by the "Crewmates Win" End Screen]**
> "Because the Impostor was successfully ejected, the game ends immediately and the Crewmates win! Our custom Node.js and Socket.io backend handles all the connections, voting logic, and dynamic code compilation seamlessly."
> 
> "That is CodeMongUs. Thank you!"

---

## 💡 Pro-Tips for recording this:
* **The Transition:** Start by recording just one browser window maximized. When you say the line *"I'm going to share a split-screen view"*, that is your cue in your video editor to magically wipe or transition to the split-screen layout! It looks super professional.
* **Cursor Movement:** While talking about the real-time syncing, use both hands (if you have two mice/trackpads) or quickly switch between windows to make both cursors dance on the screen. Judges love seeing the cursors move at the exact same time.
